Wind Solar Robot

Skills

  • SolidWorks CAD
  • Hand Calculations
  • Material Selection
  • Design Iteration
  • Testing

For the 2023 ASME Student Design Competition, I helped design and build a remote-controlled robot capable of harnessing both solar and wind energy to move a weighted payload within a time limit. The challenge required balancing power generation, storage efficiency, and drivetrain performance—all within tight energy constraints. Our team's design placed 1st overall, outperforming approximately twenty university teams.

My role focused on testing and optimization, where I conducted controlled experiments to quantify power generation and conversion efficiency under varying light and airflow conditions. Using hand calculations and Excel-based data analysis, I determined optimal operating parameters for the onboard solar panels and turbines, ensuring consistent voltage and torque output throughout the run cycle. I also assisted in refining component placement to maximize energy capture during simultaneous solar and wind exposure.

Through iterative testing and performance tuning, we achieved a system that generated peak power output and efficient energy utilization, allowing the robot to carry the highest payload distance among competitors. The project reinforced my skills in experimental validation, data-driven optimization, and practical energy systems design, bridging theory with real-world performance engineering.
