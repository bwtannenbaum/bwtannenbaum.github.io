UAV Payload Mechanism

Skills

  • SolidWorks CAD
  • Hand Calculations
  • Material Selection
  • Design Iteration
  • Testing

As part of my senior capstone project, I co-engineered a UAV-mounted claw system designed to remotely deploy and retrieve payloads from salt marsh environments. The goal was to streamline ecological data collection by allowing drones to safely place and recover audio recording units (ARUs) without manual intervention in difficult terrain.

I designed the protective payload housing and claw mechanism in SolidWorks, selecting PETG for its UV and saltwater resistance. Through hand calculations and design validation, I established requirements for torque, power draw, and battery capacity to ensure reliable operation within drone limits.

Functional and drop testing guided iterative improvements, leading to a design capable of safely securing payloads dropped from up to 8 feet and improving retrieval success rates by approximately 70% from heights above 5 feet. The final prototype met all mechanical load, torque, and current draw specifications and demonstrated strong proof-of-concept performance for future UAV integration.
