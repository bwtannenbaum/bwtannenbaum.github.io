About Me

Mechanical Engineer

Ben Tannenbaum

I have a strong passion for rapid prototyping, CAD design, and hands-on problem solving. I enjoy taking ideas from concept to production through 3D printing, testing, and iteration—turning designs into functional, manufacturable parts.

I'm driven by curiosity and a desire to continuously improve—whether that means refining a drone component, optimizing a mechanical assembly, or experimenting with new fabrication techniques.

Skills

Mechanical Design

I design and develop mechanical systems and assemblies using CAD-based workflows. My focus is on creating manufacturable designs optimized for manufacturability, ease of assembly, and weight.

Rapid Prototyping

I specialize in turning CAD models into functional prototypes through 3D printing, machining, and hands-on testing. I enjoy refining designs through iterative testing, focusing on improving fit, durability, and overall system performance.

Simulation & Analysis

Using tools like ANSYS Mechanical and MATLAB, I analyze component performance under real-world conditions. My experience includes modal and stress analysis, vibration testing, and system identification for dynamic models such as spring-mass-damper and pendulum systems.

Robotics & Controls

I've worked on visualizing and testing robotic systems using Python-based tools like Meshcat, applying forward kinematics and rotation matrices to simulate and analyze motion. I enjoy combining mechanics and computation to bring movement to life in real time.
